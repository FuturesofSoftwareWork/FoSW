AI tools—especially GenAI—are currently causing possibly even fundamental changes in most every software company and software development organization. In essence, agility is about sensing and responding to changes. Consequently, there is increasing need for software development organizations to be agile not only in software development teams (software agility) but at all organizational levels and dimensions. That calls for business, organizational, and enterprise agility to adopt modern AI tools intelligently for effective and sustainable outcomes. Then, such successful AI adoption may in turn support and strengthen agility capabilities further.

## AI Adoption in Software Development and Organizations

In established software development organizations, software work has been conducted previously with various process models and workflow arrangements. In principle, every software organization has some actual software development processes and workflows which may or may not have been defined fully explicitly. In addition, they may occasionally vary—perhaps even unconsciously—depending on the current circumstances. Nevertheless, every time new software is created and delivered, the organization has followed some processes and workflows—at least ad hoc or accidental.

Now that various AI tools have been and are taken into use, they in practice affect those software processes and workflows in one way or another. They may change the ways of doing various software engineering tasks (currently especially coding), they may make some software engineering tasks unnecessary, and they may introduce new necessary tasks and activities (e.g., prompt management).

In all, it is crucial to realize how such various changes affect the software work across the organization vertically and horizontally—all the way from the product development inception to the customer/user delivery and evolution. Where are business and customer value created and how do new AI technology adoptions influence value creation? For instance, if software developers with AI assistant tools can produce software code faster than before, what is the net-effect in terms of customer satisfaction? Value Stream Mapping (VSM) and systems thinking may provide means to realize and make such effects visible.

## Agility For Successful AI Adoption

For many current software development companies and organizations, new AI tools are technological changes coming from external environment. Agility is needed to sense and respond to such changes effectively and appropriately. As AI technology is currently changing fast and occasionally perhaps even radically, timely responses even at the enterprise level may be necessary just to remain competitive. All that calls for business, organizational and enterprise agility capabilities.

To begin with, agility requires continuous scanning and sensing of potential changes in the business and technological environments, possibly even anticipating them. In case of AI technology, especially the current rapid development of the different AI tools and their versions (e.g., LLMs) stress the organization to stay continuously aware of what is currently available and possibly coming up soon.

Then, responding to the external changes effectively and timely may require many kinds of actions in the organization. For instance, if AI-assistants for coding enable software developers to produce software faster, an agile software organization capable of organizational agility may decide to reallocate the developers even totally differently throughout the organization. With business agility, it may realize that such reallocations may allow developing additional new products. Enterprise agility makes the moves managed across the organization and aligned with the overall strategic directions.
